Coq au vin

Coq au Vin is the well-known French chicken stew where pieces of meat are braised in a luscious, glossy red wine sauce with bacon, mushroom and onions. Like Beef Bourguignon, the beauty of this dish lies in its simplicity. There are remarkably few ingredients and it’s a simple process, but the results are fit for a king – or queen!

Instructions:

  1. Marinate the Chicken (Optional, but Recommended):
    • Place the chicken in a bowl and pour the red wine over it.
    • Add bay leaves and thyme. Cover and refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight.
  2. Prepare Ingredients:
    • Remove the chicken from the marinade and pat it dry. Reserve the wine.
    • In a large pot or Dutch oven, cook the bacon until crispy. Remove and set aside.
  3. Brown the Chicken:
    • In the same pot, add olive oil and butter. Brown the chicken pieces on all sides (about 5 minutes per side). Remove and set aside.
  4. Cook the Aromatics:
    • Add chopped onion and garlic to the pot, cooking until softened
    • Stir in the flour and cook for 1-2 minutes to form a roux.
  5. Simmer the Stew:
    • Return the chicken to the pot. Pour in the reserved wine and chicken broth.
    • Add tomato paste, bay leaves, and thyme. Stir well.
    • Cover and let simmer on low heat for 45 minutes to 1 hour until the chicken is tender.
  6. Prepare Mushrooms and Pearl Onions:
    • In a separate pan, sauté mushrooms and pearl onions in butter until golden.
    • Add them to the pot for the last 15 minutes of cooking.
  7. Final Touches:
    • Stir in the cooked bacon and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper.
    • Remove bay leaves and thyme sprigs before serving.
  8. Serve:
    • Garnish with chopped parsley. Serve hot with crusty bread, mashed potatoes, or buttered noodles.

Ingredients

  • 1 whole chicken (cut into 8 pieces) or 4 bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs and 4 drumsticks
  • 200g (7 oz) bacon or pancetta, diced
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 2 cups (500ml) dry red wine (Burgundy or Pinot Noir)
  • 1 cup (250ml) chicken broth
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 tbsp flour
  • 200g (7 oz) button mushrooms, halved
  • 12 pearl onions, peeled
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 4 sprigs of thyme
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Chopped parsley (for garnish)
